Pilau

Ingridients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 4 cups water or broth (chicken or vegetable)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1-2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1-2 teaspoons cumin seeds
  • 1-2 teaspoons coriander powder
  • 1-2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1-2 whole cloves
  • 1-2 cardamom pods
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. The Rice: Wash the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak it in water for about 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. The Oil: In a large pot or pan, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat.
  3. Aromatics: Add cumin seeds, cloves, cardamom pods, and bay leaf. Saut√© for a minute until fragrant. Then add chopped onions and cook until golden brown.
  4. Add Garlic and Ginger: Stir in the minced garlic and grated ginger, cooking for another minute.
  5. Tomatoes and Spices: Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft. Mix in the coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook for a few more minutes until everything is well combined.
  6. Add Rice: Stir in the drained rice, ensuring it's well coated with the spices. Cook for a couple of minutes, allowing it to toast slightly.
  7. Add Liquid: Pour in the water or broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  8. Fluff and Serve: Once done, turn off the heat and let it sit covered for another 5-10 minutes. Fluff the rice with a fork and serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro if desired.
