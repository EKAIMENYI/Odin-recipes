Rice Recipe

Ingridients

  1. 1 cup rice (white or brown)
  2. 2 cups water (for white rice) or 2.5 cups water (for brown rice)
  3. Salt (optional)
  4. Butter or oil (optional)

Instructions

  • Rinse the Rice: Place the rice in a fine-mesh sieve or bowl. Rinse it under cold running water until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch and prevents the rice from becoming gummy.
  • Soak (Optional): Soaking the rice for 30 minutes can help improve the texture, especially for long-grain varieties.
  • Measure Water: For white rice, use a 2:1 ratio (2 cups of water for every 1 cup of rice). For brown rice, use a 2.5:1 ratio.
  • Boil Water: In a pot, bring the measured water to a boil. If desired, add salt and a tablespoon of butter or oil for flavor.
  • Add Rice: Once the water is boiling, add the rinsed rice. Stir briefly to prevent sticking.
  • Cover and Simmer: Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with a lid, and let it simmer. For white rice, cook for about 15-20 minutes; for brown rice, about 40-45 minutes.
  • Check for Doneness: After the cooking time, check if all the water has been absorbed. If the rice is tender, it’s done. If there’s still water, cover and cook for a few more minutes.
  • Fluff the Rice: Once cooked, remove the pot from heat. Let it sit for 5 minutes, then fluff the rice gently with a fork.
  • Serve: Serve the rice as a side dish or base for your favorite main course.
